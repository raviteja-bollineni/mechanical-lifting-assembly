# Mechanical Lifting Assembly Design

This project presents a mechanical lifting assembly developed for a design task requiring a steel payload to be lifted by 500 mm at a minimum speed of 100 mm/s.

## Project Objective

The objective was to design a compact lifting system that could mount only to the existing M10 tapped bosses on the supplied base frame. The assembly needed to lift the payload safely, maintain alignment during travel, and meet the required lifting speed.

## Design Solution

The final design uses a motor-driven lead screw mechanism with dual guide rails for stability. A cantilevered bridge plate positions the lifting axis over the payload zone, while the guide rails reduce twisting and racking during motion.

## Key Design Features

* Motor-driven lead screw lifting mechanism
* 1605 lead screw with 5 mm pitch
* Motor A selection
* 15 mm bridge plate
* Dual 20 mm guide rails for stability
* Lifting carriage connected to the payload
* Mounting through the four M10 bosses on the base frame

## Performance Calculations

* Required lift height: 500 mm
* Required lift speed: ≥ 100 mm/s
* Payload mass: 31.6 kg
* Required lifting force: approximately 310 N
* Lead screw pitch: 5 mm
* Motor speed used for calculation: 1200 RPM

Lift speed calculation:

```text
V = (RPM × Pitch) / 60
V = (1200 × 5) / 60
V = 100 mm/s
```

## Outcome

The design meets the required lifting height and speed using standard industrial components. The guide rail system improves stability, while the lead screw provides controlled vertical motion for the payload.
